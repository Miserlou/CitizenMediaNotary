Citizen Media Notary


CitizenMediaNotary is a distributed network of servers which temporally verify the authenticity of documents as they are produced. Our goal is to help citizens, documentarians, lawyers, publishers, authors, businesses and consumers temporally and cryptographically prove the authenticy of the documents they produce.


How?

Whenever a new document is produced and presented to the network, a record of the fingerprint of that document is verified, stored, signed, and distributed around the globe to the rest of the network for verification.

To assess the history of a document, search for the name or cryptographic hash of the file, and you'll get the network's record for that file, thus proving the record's temporal authenticity.

Who Can Use It?

CitizenMediaNotary can be used by anybody who wants the media they produce to be verified.

  • Citizen Documentarians, who want to use their recordings in news articles
  • Lawyers, who want to prove the authenticy of their evidence in court
  • Businesses, who want to prove a record of their contracts
  • Officials, who want to verify their public record

CMN is filetype agnostic, so it can work for lots of things, like..

  • Cell phone audio/video recordings
  • Emails / Chat and IRC Logs
  • Contracts
  • Published Articles

More Information

CMN is developed by The OpenWatch Project for use with our citizen media software (in conjunction with the National Lawyers Guild and the ACLU), but it can be used for a variety of purposes.
